---
title: "Capacity Building Grants Program"
mission: "To strengthen the nonprofit sector by investing in organizational capacity. The Capacity Building Grants Program provides funding to help nonprofits build long-term organizational health through projects that improve systems, processes, and leadership."
eligibility: |
  - Must be a 501(c)(3) organization
  - Located in Allegheny County
  - Annual operating budget under $5 million
  - Must demonstrate a clear capacity building need tied to your strategic goals
whatYouGet: |
  - **Grant funding** of $5,000–$25,000
  - **Consultant access** through the Forbes Funds consultant network
  - **Peer learning cohort** with other grantees facing similar challenges
  - **Technical assistance** throughout the grant period
howItWorks: |
  1. **Submit a Letter of Intent** — During the open application period, describe your organization and the capacity building project you have in mind.
  2. **Full Proposal Invitation** — Selected organizations are invited to submit a detailed proposal with budget and timeline.
  3. **Independent Review** — Proposals are evaluated by an independent panel based on need, feasibility, and potential impact.
  4. **Award & Kickoff** — Awards are announced within 60 days. Grantees join the peer learning cohort and are matched with consultants.
proofPoints: |
  - **150+** organizations funded since inception
  - **92%** of grantees report improved organizational systems
  - **$15K** average grant size
faq: |
  **Can we use grant funds for general operating support?**
  Grants must be tied to a specific capacity building project, but projects can include staff time dedicated to capacity building work.

  **How long is the grant period?**
  Grant periods are typically 12 months from the award date.

  **Can we apply for more than one Forbes Funds program at a time?**
  Yes. Each program has its own application process and timeline. We encourage organizations to explore all programs that fit their needs.
contactEmail: "emily@forbesfunds.org"
---

Grants range from $5,000 to $25,000 and support projects that create lasting improvements in how organizations operate and serve their communities.
